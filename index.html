The idea

One cycle, three signals

↕

Ground reaction force

The force the ground pushes back on your body with, in multiples of body weight (BW). Every push you make, the ground makes right back.

⟲

Joint angles

How much the hip, knee, and ankle flex and extend through the movement cycle — the kinematic signature of each task.

⚡

Muscle activation

Which muscle groups switch on and off, and how hard, to control and power the movement — based on classic EMG findings.

Curves are illustrative reconstructions based on well-established biomechanics literature (see Sources), not raw patient data — built to teach the pattern, not to diagnose an individual.

Sources

Most curve shapes, magnitudes, and timing are illustrative reconstructions informed by these widely cited biomechanics references — not a substitute for individual assessment. The one exception is running muscle activation, which is computed directly from real EMG (see Santuz et al. below).

  • Perry J, Burnfield JM. Gait Analysis: Normal and Pathological Function, 2nd ed. SLACK Incorporated, 2010.
  • Novacheck TF. The biomechanics of running. Gait & Posture, 1998.
  • Santuz A, Ekizos A, Janshen L, Baltzopoulos V, Arampatzis A. Modular control of human movement during running: an open access data set. Front. Physiol., 2018. Zenodo data set — the running muscle-activation curves here are the grand-average EMG envelopes computed from this data set's raw signals (11,388 gait cycles, 135 adults).
  • Winter DA. Biomechanics and Motor Control of Human Movement, 4th ed. Wiley, 2009.
  • Bobbert MF, van Ingen Schenau GJ. Coordination in vertical jumping. J Biomech, 1988.
  • Escamilla RF. Knee biomechanics of the dynamic squat exercise. Med Sci Sports Exerc, 2001.
  • Decker MJ, Torry MR, Wyland DJ, Sterett WI, Steadman JR. Gender differences in lower extremity kinematics, kinetics and energy absorption during landing. Clin Biomech, 2003.
  • McErlain-Naylor S, King M, Pain MTG. Determinants of countermovement jump performance: a kinetic and kinematic analysis. J Sports Sci, 2014.
